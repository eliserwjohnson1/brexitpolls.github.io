Know the facts on the EU

The referendum for the UK leaving the EU has been labeled a "Once in a generation choice by the Prime Minister. This website's aim is to provide our audience with unbiased knowledge. We are here to give you all the facts at your fingertips. With panicked statements of the UK eventually become slaves to a united states of europe or the promise that if the UK leaves the world will go bankrupt, it feels like their is a lot of pressure on the average UK citizen. While we will be unable to vote for you, we can at least give you an indication of what other people are voting.

What is everyone else thinking?

Often humans surround Themselves with like minded people, therefore you may believe that most people of your generation and gender agree with your perspective. Put this to the test with our interactive toy. Enter in your gender and age group to find out what percentages of people like you will be voting to remain, leave or still don’t know. Interestingly people who are 65 years + are most in favour to leave, and the make up a large percentage of the voting population, with 79% of them voting in 2015. While 18-24 year olds, arguably the most affected by the EU referendum as they will be living with the decision for longer, have 58% wanting to remain. However, if the voting patterns remain the same as 2015 and only 43% turnout to vote their voice might be drowned out by the older generations.



Where does your Mp stand on Brexit?

Whether your Mp voted to remain in or leave the EU may effect your opinion of them in future elections. To use this interactive toy simply type in your postcode then your constituency and Mp will come up. The most surprising Mp we found was Zac Goldsmith, who is currently running for London Mayor. He has declared he wants to leave the EU, which is a bold move considering the majority Londoners are in favour of remaining in the EU.
